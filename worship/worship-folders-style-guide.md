# Worship Folders Style Guide

Please note, this is a work in progress.

## Purpose

## Document Structure

### Front Cover

Every worship folder must have a cover and include some basic information about Victory and the service.

#### Front Cover Sections

Each cover should include:

|Section                |Description|
|-----------------------|---|
|**Cover image**        |The cover image is based on the current worship series.|
|**Service theme**      |The service theme is based on the current worship series or, for special services (e.g. festivals, installations, etc.), describes the purpose of the service.|
|**Service holiday**    |The service holiday is based on the liturgical calendar.|
|**Service date**       |The service date is the calendar date of the service.|
|**Victory logo**       |The Victory logo on the front cover may include the full logo, the "Victory-only" version, or the "cross-only" version.|
|**Victory information**|Victory information on the front cover should include:<ul><li>Victory's Mission Statement</li><li>Victory's address</li><li>Victory's phone number</li></ul>|

### Welcome Page

It is very strongly recommended that every worship folder have a welcome page which includes a welcome message, as well as some more detailed information about Victory and the worship facilities.

#### Welcome Page Sections

The welcome page should include:

|Section                                 |Description|
|----------------------------------------|---|
|**Tagline**                             |A large heading with a short and simple welcome message.<br><br>*Example taglines:*<ul><li>"A Place to Call Home: Victory Lutheran"</li><li>"Welcome to Victory!"</li></ul><br>*Avoid descriptive or neutral taglines:*<ul><li>"Victory Lutheran Church"</li><li>"Service and Facility Information"</li></ul>|
|**Welcome Message**                     |A paragraph-length message welcoming the reader to Victory, with a special emphasis on welcoming guests and a prayer for their edification through the worship service.|
|**Quick Notes**                         |Short, one-to-two-line notes informing the reader about...<ul><li>the connection card, including a request that they fill one out.</li><li>the restrooms, including available facilities and location.</li><li>the nursery (or quiet room), including information about its purpose and location.</li><li>Victory's online presence, including website and social media pages. Social media brand logos may be included as well to catch the reader's eye.</li></ul>|
|**Worship Series Title and Description**|The heading for this section is made up of the preheading "Worship Series:" (or "Worship Series Focus:") followed by the title of the worship series.<br>Below the section heading is the description for the worship series.|
|**Service Theme Title and Description** |The heading for this section is made up of the preheading "Today's Worship:" followed by the theme of the service.<br>Below the section heading is the description for the service theme.|

### Worship Path

The worship path is the main part of the worship folder and includes the full liturgy for the worship service.

#### Worship Path Sections

Generally, the worship path includes the following sections:

|Section                 |Description|
|------------------------|---|
|**Service Introduction**|Usually, this section includes:<ul><li>the invocation</li><li>opening sentences (optional)</li><li>the opening hymn</li><li>confession of sins</li><li>the *Kyrie*</li><li>the *Gloria in Excelsis*</li></ul>|
|**The Word**            |Usually, this section includes:<ul><li>the Prayer of the Day</li><li>the First Reading</li><li>the Psalm of the Day (optional)</li><li>the Second Reading</li><li>a Gospel Acclamation or responsive sentences (optional)</li><li>the Gospel Reading</li><li>the children's message</li><li>a note about Children's Church</li><li>the Hymn of the Day</li><li>the sermon</li><li>the offering</li><li>the *Credo*</li><li>the Prayer of the Church</li><li>the Lord's Prayer[^1]</li><li>the blessing[^1]</li><li>the closing hymn[^1]</li></ul>|
|**The Sacrament**       |Usually this section includes:<ul><li>the Preface</li><li>the Lord's Prayer[^1]</li><li>the *Sanctus*</li><li>the Words of Institution</li><li>the *Agnus Dei*</li><li>the Prayer of Thanksgiving (optional)</li><li>the distribution note</li><li>the distribution hymn</li></ul>|
|**Thanksgiving**        |The section includes:<ul><li>responisive thanksigiving sentences</li><li>a prayer</li><li>the blessing[^1]</li><li>the closing hymn[^1]</li></ul>|

### Post-Service Page

The post-service page is the place for service-related information as well as announcements, note pages, and upcoming services.

#### Post-Service Page Sections

Though it's referred to here as a "page", the post-service "page" should begin immediately after the end of the worship path. This section should include:

|Section                          |Description|
|---------------------------------|---|
|**Post-Worship Activities/Notes**|This could include a video, such as a WELS Connection or Kids Connection or a special report or opportunity for recognition.|
|**Serving in Worship**           |This section lists the volunteers in worship, usually limited to the pastor and musicians, though it may also include lectors and other volunteers in worship.|
|**Announcements**                |This section is flexible in its contents and layout, though a suggested format is listed below.|
|**Acknowledgements**             |This includes copyright any other related information.|
|**Note Pages**                   |These are optional and are used for space-filling purposes.|
|**Upcoming Services**            |This section is optional and is used for space-filling. It includes artwork for upcoming worship series as well as service dates and themes.|
|**Study Questions**              |This section is optional and may include study questions optionally based on the sermon or readings.|

## Layout & Spacing

The service introduction usually does not have a heading, though it may. The section entitled "The Word" should have a heading when possible, though it may be omitted to save space. The section entitled "The Sacrament" should have a heading when possible. While the heading may be omitted to save space, it is recommended to try saving space elsewhere first. The section entitled "Thanksgiving" should have a heading when possible, though it may be omitted to save space.
