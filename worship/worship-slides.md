# Worship Slides

## Creating a New Slide Deck

To create a new deck of worship slides,

1. access the worship slides folder in the [Google Shared Drive](../google/accessing-the-shared-drive.md) (Worship > Worship Slides).
2. Right click on the slide deck whose theme matches that of the service you are preparing.
3. Right click on the newly created slide deck, rename it, then double click to open it.

## Making the Slide Deck

1. Once the copied slide deck is opened, delete all slides except for the title and endcard slides.
    ::: info Reusing slides
    While you could reuse slides from the copied service, it is recommended to start fresh to ensure no content from the previous service is accidentally left behind.
    :::

2. Fill in the appropriate information on the title and endcard slides.

3. To add a new slide, click the plus button in the upper left corner of the editing ribbon, or press Ctrl/Cmd + M.
    ::: info Slide Layout
    By default, the new slide will match the layout of the previous slide. To select a different layout, ensure no elements within the slide are selected and click "Layout" in the editing ribbon.
    :::

4. Copy text from the finalized bulletin and paste it onto the slide in the appropriate text box.
    ::: tip Tip
    Using your operating system's or browser's "Paste as plain text" functionality can save a great amount of editing time.
    :::

5. Continue to copy and paste text from the bulletin into the slide deck for the rest of the service.
    ::: info Hymn Lyrics
    Hymn lyrics can be found in the bulletin portion of [Service Builder](./worship-folders.md#service-builder) by setting the hymn style to "Text", right clicking, and choosing "Copy".
    :::

## General Notes and Tips

### Responsive Text and Responsorials

Always make sure text spoken or sung by the congregation is bolded, including text spoken by both the pastor and the congregation. Additionally, since all congregational text is bold, there is no need for responsorials ("Minister:", "Congregation:", "All:", etc.), even if they are present in the worship folder.

### Canticles and Verse Markings

Nearly every slide layout includes a small text box on the far left, partially overlapped by the main body text box. Such text boxes are used in two situations:

1. **Canticles:** songs like "Glory to God in the Highest", "Holy, Holy, Holy", etc. which do not usually have verse numbers and do not use the "Hymn Title" layout for their title slide. For canticles, the small text box on the left is used to signal to the congregation that the text will be sung rather than spoken. Simply insert a beamed eighth notes (♫) symbol and align it vertically with the first line of text. The symbol does not need to be used for subsequent slides in the same canticle.
    ::: info Inserting the ♫ Character
    To insert the beamed eighth notes character, click "Insert" in the menu bar, then click "Ω&nbsp;Special&nbsp;Characters". In the search box that appears, type "266b", then press enter. Click the "♫" symbol to insert it.
    :::

2. **Hymns:** songs which include verse numbers. For these songs, the small text box on the left is used to mark verses. Simply input the verse number on the first slide of the verse and ensure it is vertically aligned with the first line of text in the verse.

In both cases: canticles and hymns, the main body text box should be narrowed from the left side to ensure the edges of the main body text box and the small text box on the left are aligned.

### Titles and Subtitles

Many parts of the worship folder have both titles and subtitles. For parts that have both, a slide layout should be chosen which includes both. Sometimes, it is straightforward to know which text should be used for the title and which for the subtitle. For example, the Latin and Greek names of the parts of the service ("Kyrie", "Gloria in Excelsis", "Sanctus", etc.) always take the place of the subtitle.

In general, the title corresponds to what that section is, whereas the subtitle corresponds to what role that section has in the service. However, this is not always the case. For example, the Gospel Acclamation is often based on a verse from the upcoming gospel reading. However, on the Gospel Acclamation slide, the reference is used as the subtitle and the role (Gospel Acclamation) is used as the main title (except if the acclamation is a named song).

To know which part in the worship folder matches up with which text box in the slides, simply ask the following question: does the entirety of this section in the worship folder compose the title or the subtitle? For example, though the Gospel Acclamation includes a passage from the upcoming gospel reading and that passage's reference is the subtitle in the worship folder, because the acclamation also includes a refrain, it is more than just the reference. Therefore, we use the passage as the subtitle and the role (Gospel Acclamation) as the title. Another example: let's say we have responsive sentences from Psalm 119. In the worship folder, the title is "Responsive Sentences" and the subtitle is "Psalm 119". Clearly, the responsive sentences will not be the entirety of Psalm 119 because of its length, so the reference (Psalm 119) will be used as the subtitle, and the role (Responsive Senteces) will be used as the title. As a counterexample: oftentimes in the worship folder, the creed is titled "Confession of Faith" and the name of the creed is listed as the subtitle. However, because the entirety of the section *is* the named creed, that is, we speak all the words from the creed and not just part of it, the name of the creed is used as the title of the slide and the role (Confession of Faith) is used as the subtitle. However, if we are speaking only a part of the creed, for example, when we speak excerpts of the Athanasian Creed, then the entirety of the section is not the entirety of the Athanasian Creed, so the name of the creed (The Athanasian Creed) would be the subtitle and the role (Confession of Faith) would be the title.

### Reading Text

The majority of slide layouts include text boxes that are already sized so that the text will not overlap the series logo in the lower right corner. The exception is the reading text layout, recognizable by its vertically split format. When creating slides using this layout, ensure text does not overlap around the series logo.
